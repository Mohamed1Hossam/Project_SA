The Tonale plug-in hybrid features a turbocharged, 1.3-liter four-cylinder and an electric motor mounted on the rear axle that together generate a combined 285 horses and provide all-wheel drive. Alfa equips the plug-in Tonale with a six-speed automatic and a 12.0-kWh lithium-ion battery pack that provides an EPA-estimated 33-mile electric-only driving range. All models ride on a fully independent suspension; adaptive dampers are optional as part of the Veloce package. During our most recent test, we came away disappointed with the Tonale's road manners. Alfa's other models set a high bar, and the Tonale is simply not as engaging as the Giulia and Stelvio, with steering that’s too isolated from the road for our liking. The plug-in-hybrid powertrain provides good around-town punch and solid braking performance but there’s no magic in the way the Tonale drives, unlike Alfa’s other driver-friendly models.
